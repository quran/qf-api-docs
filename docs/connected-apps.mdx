steps, not as one automatic approval.

## What Users See On Quran.com/apps

The public <a href="https://quran.com/apps">Quran.com/apps</a> page is designed
for user discovery, not for internal review history. It can show featured apps,
browseable app cards, categories, icons, platform links, taglines, and short
descriptions.

Directory cards should help users answer five questions quickly:

<div className="connectedAppsGrid connectedAppsGridTwo">
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>User questions the listing should answer</h3>
    <ul className="connectedAppsChecklist">
      <li>What does this app help me do?</li>
      <li>Who owns and supports it?</li>
      <li>Which platforms can I use it on?</li>
      <li>Does it connect to my Quran.com account or request data access?</li>
      <li>Where can I read its privacy, support, and product information?</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>What a directory card is not</h3>
    <ul className="connectedAppsChecklist">
      <li>It is not a full marketing page for the app.</li>
      <li>It is not a promise of homepage placement or traffic.</li>
      <li>It does not mean Quran Foundation owns or supports the app.</li>
      <li>It does not mean every current and future feature has been endorsed.</li>
      <li>It does not replace the app owner's own privacy, terms, and support obligations.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
</div>

## Visibility Paths And Labels

Visibility is not a single switch. Directory listing, reviewed labels, account
connection, and featured placement each answer a different user need.

  <table className="connectedAppsTable">
    <thead>
      <tr>
        <th>Path</th>
        <th>What Quran Foundation reviews</th>
        <th>What the app may say</th>
        <th>What the app must not imply</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Listed</strong></td>
        <td>Complete listing package, working destination links, ownership and support contacts, lawful product, clear purpose, and basic quality fit.</td>
        <td>The app is listed in the Quran.com Connected Apps directory.</td>
        <td>Quran Foundation ownership, guaranteed traffic, homepage exposure, or endorsement of every feature.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Verified or aligned</strong></td>
        <td>The specific content, quality, source, attribution, mission, or relationship checks defined for that label.</td>
        <td>The app has completed the checks described for that label.</td>
        <td>A broad guarantee that every product area, future release, or partner claim is approved.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Connected</strong></td>
        <td>OAuth readiness, OAuth consent copy, redirect URIs, proportionate scopes, privacy information, data security, incident contact, and support route.</td>
        <td>The app can connect to approved Quran Foundation account or user-data features.</td>
        <td>Permission to request unnecessary scopes, bypass consent, or reuse data outside the approved purpose.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Featured</strong></td>
        <td>User value, quality, timing, audience fit, support readiness, current availability, and suitability for the placement.</td>
        <td>The app is featured in a specific Quran Foundation surface or campaign while that placement is active.</td>
        <td>Permanent status, paid advertising, ranking guarantees, or an ownership relationship.</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

## Listing Package

A listing package should make the product understandable, reviewable, and safe
to present to users. Incomplete or unclear packages slow down review.

<div className="connectedAppsGrid connectedAppsGridTwo">
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Required for review</h3>
    <ul className="connectedAppsChecklist">
      <li>Product name, short purpose statement, and primary category.</li>
      <li>Square logo or icon with safe contrast and confirmed source rights.</li>
      <li>Working web, iOS, and/or Android destination links.</li>
      <li>Product owner, technical contact, support contact, and public support route.</li>
      <li>Privacy notice and terms where applicable.</li>
      <li>Clear explanation of Quran Foundation content, authentication, scopes, or user-data use.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Recommended for a stronger listing</h3>
    <ul className="connectedAppsChecklist">
      <li>One plain-language differentiator.</li>
      <li>Platform and language availability.</li>
      <li>High-quality screenshots or preview media.</li>
      <li>Accessibility and age-appropriateness notes.</li>
      <li>Accurate content attribution where surfaced in product.</li>
      <li>Current release, maintenance, and support status.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
</div>

## Review Criteria

Review is contextual. The same product can be suitable for a directory listing
but not for OAuth scopes, homepage placement, or a reviewed label.

<div className="connectedAppsGrid connectedAppsGridThree">
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Mission and usefulness</h3>
    <p>
      Does the product solve a real user need and support Quran reading,
      listening, understanding, memorization, reflection, or continuity?
    </p>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Content and attribution</h3>
    <p>
      Are Quranic text, translations, tafsir, recitations, and derived content
      sourced, represented, and attributed accurately?
    </p>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Data and consent</h3>
    <p>
      Are requested scopes proportionate, explained clearly, secured
      appropriately, and limited to the product function?
    </p>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Product quality</h3>
    <p>
      Does the product work reliably, avoid avoidable confusion, and provide a
      support route for users who need help?
    </p>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Commercial model</h3>
    <p>
      Are charges, ads, donations, sponsorships, or affiliate models
      transparent, proportionate, and compatible with content obligations?
    </p>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Ongoing accountability</h3>
    <p>
      Can the app owner keep listing details current, respond to issues, and
      cooperate with reasonable review or corrective action?
    </p>
  </article>
</div>

## User-Facing Clarity

These expectations are for app owners and partners. Communicate them to users
where they matter: in the app, listing copy, consent screens, privacy notices,
terms, onboarding, support pages, or release notes.

<div className="connectedAppsGrid connectedAppsGridTwo">
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Make clear to users</h3>
    <ul className="connectedAppsChecklist">
      <li>Who owns and operates the app.</li>
      <li>Where users should go for product support.</li>
      <li>What Quran Foundation content, APIs, or account features the app uses.</li>
      <li>What data or permissions are requested and why they are needed.</li>
      <li>Whether AI, ads, donations, purchases, or sponsorships are part of the experience.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Avoid misleading claims</h3>
    <ul className="connectedAppsChecklist">
      <li>Do not present an independent app as Quran Foundation owned unless it is.</li>
      <li>Do not describe a directory listing as broad endorsement of the product.</li>
      <li>Do not stretch a reviewed label beyond the checks described for that label.</li>
      <li>Do not imply homepage placement, featured status, or ranking is guaranteed.</li>
      <li>Do not hide material changes to data use, monetization, AI behavior, or content sources.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
</div>

## Responsible Use

Products that handle Quranic material, user progress, religious learning, or AI
assistance need extra clarity. Build trust before asking for visibility.

<div className="connectedAppsGrid connectedAppsGridTwo">
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Build toward trust</h3>
    <ul className="connectedAppsChecklist">
      <li>Present Quranic material accurately and in appropriate context.</li>
      <li>Design for dignity, accessibility, and informed user choices.</li>
      <li>Make AI-assisted features clear about their limits.</li>
      <li>Separate exploratory or generated output from authoritative religious guidance.</li>
      <li>Use only the scopes, content fields, and data flows your product truly needs.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Avoid avoidable harm</h3>
    <ul className="connectedAppsChecklist">
      <li>Do not use Quranic material as a superficial sales hook.</li>
      <li>Avoid intrusive advertising, manipulative retention, or ambiguous sponsorship.</li>
      <li>Do not over-collect user data or hide permission consequences.</li>
      <li>Do not present verified source material and unverified output as the same kind of authority.</li>
      <li>Do not use Quran Foundation branding, content, or user data outside approved scope.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
</div>

## Changes That Can Require Re-Review

Keep listing, privacy, support, and integration details current. Material
changes can require updated review before continued listing, labels, scopes, or
featured placement.

<div className="connectedAppsGrid connectedAppsGridThree">
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Product or ownership changes</h3>
    <p>
      New owner, major repositioning, discontinued support, broken destination
      links, or a change in the primary user experience.
    </p>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Data or permission changes</h3>
    <p>
      New OAuth scopes, expanded user-data use, changed consent copy, new
      processors, or security and incident-response changes.
    </p>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Content or AI changes</h3>
    <p>
      New Quranic sources, generated religious content, AI explanations,
      changes to attribution, or mixing reviewed sources with unreviewed output.
    </p>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Commercial changes</h3>
    <p>
      New ads, subscriptions, purchases, donations, sponsorships, affiliate
      links, or paid access around Quranic content or user features.
    </p>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Listing changes</h3>
    <p>
      New claims, screenshots, categories, platform links, app descriptions, or
      relationship statements shown to Quran.com users.
    </p>
  </article>
  <article className="connectedAppsCard">
    <h3>Visibility changes</h3>
    <p>
      Quran Foundation may update, pause, or remove directory visibility,
      labels, connected access, or featured placement when requirements are no
      longer met.
    </p>
  </article>
</div>

## FAQ

<details>
  <summary>Can my app charge for a service?</summary>
  <p>
    Potentially. Monetization is evaluated in context. The product should
    provide genuine user value, describe charges honestly, respect content
    obligations, and avoid using Quranic material as a superficial marketing
    vehicle. Commercial content use may require separate written permission.
  </p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Can we show ads or accept donations?</summary>
  <p>
    Possible models should be transparent, proportionate, and respectful of the
    user experience. Intrusive, deceptive, or contextually inappropriate
    promotion can affect eligibility or visibility.
  </p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Does every listed app need OAuth?</summary>
  <p>
    No. OAuth is most important where Quran Foundation account continuity,
    personal user data, or signed-in user actions are central. Where OAuth is
    used, the app should request only the permissions it truly needs and explain
    the benefit before consent.
  </p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Is a directory card an endorsement?</summary>
  <p>
    No. A listing helps users discover an independent product. It does not mean
    Quran Foundation owns the app, provides its support, or endorses every
    current and future feature.
  </p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>What is the difference between an app partner and a content partner?</summary>
  <p>
    App partners build user-facing experiences and are reviewed for product,
    data, support, and ecosystem fit. Content partners can have additional
    source, rights, attribution, licensing, and usage considerations. The review
    tracks should be distinct when those obligations differ.
  </p>
</details>

For questions about access, scopes, listing readiness, or review status, contact
